Mars missions have been launched from Earth at regular intervals since the 1960s, aiming to explore our neighboring planet. Given the potential for liquid water - and therefore life - Mars is an attractive target.

But it's been a difficult journey, and only about half of Mars missions reach the Red Planet successfully, according to NASA. A "great galactic Ghoul" must be consuming them, a NASA correspondent and corresponding scientist joked in 1964, The Economist reported. Since the first successful flight in 1965, a number of space agencies have successfully visited Mars. NASA, the former Soviet space program, the European Space Agency (ESA) and the Indian Space Research Organization have all had previous successes.
